Paneer-Butter-Masala

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the paneer: In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and coat them well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Cashew Paste:Grind the soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste by adding a little water.
  3. Cook the Paneer:Heat a pan and add 1 tablespoon of butter. Add the marinated paneer cubes and cook until they turn golden brown. Set asid
  4. Prepare the gravy:In the same pan, add another tablespoon of butter and oil.Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Add Spice Powders: Stir in the cashew paste and cook for 2-3 minutes.Add fresh cream and mix well. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
  6. Combine Paneer and Gravy:Add the cooked paneer cubes to the gravy. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  7. Adjust Seasoning:Add salt to taste and honey or sugar if desired to balance the acidity.
  8. Simmer:Let the Paneer Butter Masala simmer on low heat for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  9. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
