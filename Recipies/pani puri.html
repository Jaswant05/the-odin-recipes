Pani puri

Ingredients

  • Semolina (Suji) - 1 cup
  • All-purpose flour (Maida) - 1/4 cup
  • Baking soda - a pinch
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed (to make a stiff dough)
  • Oil - for deep frying
  • Mint leaves - 1 cup
  • Coriander leaves - 1/2 cup
  • Green chili - 1-2 (adjust to taste)
  • Ginger - 1-inch piece
  • Tamarind pulp - 2 tablespoons
  • Roasted cumin powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Black salt - 1 teaspoon
  • Chaat masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water 3-4 cups
  • Boiled and mashed potatoes - 1 cup
  • Boiled chickpeas - 1/2 cup
  • Chopped onions - 1/2 cup
  • Sweet tamarind chutney
  • Green chutney
  • Sev (thin gram flour vermicelli)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Puris:Mix semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a stiff dough.Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into small puris. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the puris until they puff up and turn golden brown. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Pani (Spicy Water):Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chili, and ginger to a fine paste.In a large bowl, mix the paste with tamarind pulp, roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, and regular salt.Add water gradually to make a thin and spicy water. Adjust the flavors according to your taste.
  3. Assemble the Pani Puri: Make a small hole in the center of each puri.Fill the puris with mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, and any additional fillings you like.Pour the spicy water into each puri using a spoon or a small cup.Top with sweet tamarind chutney, green chutney, and sprinkle some sev on top.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the delicious Pani Puri!Remember, Pani Puri is all about the burst of flavors in your mouth, so feel free to adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your preferences.
